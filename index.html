1904

The First Subway Line Opens

The New York City Subway's first line opened on October 27, 1904, running from City Hall to 145th Street. Operated by the Interborough Rapid Transit Company (IRT), the line was an instant success, carrying 150,000 passengers on its first day of operation.

1918

Dual Contracts Expansion

The Dual Contracts agreements between the city and private operators led to a massive expansion of the subway system. New lines were built in Brooklyn, Queens, and the Bronx, tripling the system's size and establishing much of the network we know today.

1940

City Takes Control

The city unified the three separate subway systems (IRT, BMT, and IND) under public operation, creating the modern New York City Transit Authority. This marked the end of private subway operation and the beginning of the unified fare system.
